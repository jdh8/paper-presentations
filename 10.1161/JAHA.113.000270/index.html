Overview

Background
Fibrocytes are linked to various fibrotic disorders.
Do they cause myocardial fibrosis?
Result
HCM patients with diffuse fibrosis have
  • Elevated plasma SDF
  • Enhanced ability of PBMCs to differentiate into fibrocytes.
Conclusion
Fibrocytes may contribute to the pathogenesis of myocardial fibrosis.

Myocardial fibrosis and HCM

Myocardial fibrosis contributes to

  • Heart failure
  • Increased risk for arrythmias and sudden death.
Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy
Unexplained left ventricular hypertrophy
  • Non‐dilated ventricular chambers
Diffuse and focal fibroses are common features.
  • Poor prognosis
Pathogenesis not completely understood

A CMR technique to evaluate myocardial fibrosis

  • Correlated with worsening diastolic dysfunction

T1 times were sigificantly lower in HCM patients.

Limitations

  1. Postcontrast myocardial T1 times are also affected by other factors that may expand the extracellular matrix.
  2. The sample size is relatively small

Levels of evidence

  2. Cohort studies
  3. Case-control studies
